Say No to Alcohol and Drug Abuse

History

The Say No to Alcohol and Drug Abuse (SNADA), is a non-governmental organization that deals with alcohol and drug abuse. SNADA was established in 2012, it came to availability as a response to growing problems of substance abuse in the different provinces and communities of South Africa.
The population growth of South African communities, the prevalence of alcohol and drugs, the demand for substance. Treatment programs and political evolution made the organization to be available to render its services for reduction and fighting substance abuse within the communities of South Africa.
SNADA has a rich tradition of developing programs for people who are addicted and also addressing substance abuse in the early phases of addiction. Our role at SNADA is to help those who are addicted to drugs and alcohol and focus on all sectors of society, as addiction knows no barriers to aspects such as class, age, race or gender.
Today there are 18 SNADA societies in most of larger centers around the country- the are two in each of the nine provinces.
