What is SNADA?

The Say No to Alcohol and Drug Abuse (SNADA) is a non-governmental organization whose major objectives are the prevention and treatment of alcohol and drug abuse.
This organization is based on addressing alcoholism and drug dependence through the provision of specialized, accessible and developmental, prevention and treatment services to all the people of South Africa. SNADA provides individuals with an opportunity to get help through treatment processes carried at SNADA and facilitating their rehabilitation once the process is over. At SNADA we strive to bring change within the communities, individuals, families and schools in South Africa, we do this by providing people with an opportunity get motivated, inspired and guided by bringing the best motivational speakers and some of the people within the community to give a word of inspiration. We also take our patients to trips to join with other patients from other SNADA centers to show them how much interesting life can be without drugs and alcohol, mostly teenagers. The main center of SNADA is situated at Soweto Eldorado Park.

Our organization is pledged to assist teenagers and alderley people involved in substance abuse such as drugs and alcohol. We help people to give themselves a second chance to bring change into their lives and the community by setting an example of a better future.

For more information you can visit our web pages. We want to create a better future for every South African citizen who are willing to take a second chance to shape their own journey in life. At SNADA patients who have been through the treatment process have to come back after two weeks for examination and test and have to collect the result after one week.
